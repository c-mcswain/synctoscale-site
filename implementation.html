Discovery & alignment

Early implementation focuses on shared understanding. Teams align on which systems matter, what events should trigger workflows, and what outcomes define success.

System inventory

Teams typically review which tools act as sources of truth and where key changes originate (e.g., HR systems, CRMs, ticketing tools).

Ownership clarity

Roles and responsibilities are defined early so approvals, changes, and questions have clear owners throughout implementation.

Integration setup

Once scope is clear, integrations are connected using scoped credentials or OAuth. Initial setup emphasizes security, reliability, and visibility into connection health.

Credential strategy

Teams typically use service accounts or limited scopes to reduce risk and simplify future access reviews.

Early validation

Connections are tested before workflows are enabled to confirm authorization and data access behave as expected.

Workflow validation

Before rollout, workflows are reviewed using representative scenarios to confirm mappings, conditions, and guardrails produce the intended outcomes.

Mapping confidence

Field mappings and conditions are reviewed to prevent unexpected behavior when real events begin flowing.

Scenario testing

Teams validate workflows using staging or test data to surface edge cases early.

Rollout & stabilization

Rollout is typically staged. Teams observe early executions, review logs, and make adjustments before expanding coverage.

Gradual enablement

Workflows are often enabled for a subset of users or events before broader rollout.

Observation period

Logs and notifications help teams verify behavior and build confidence post-launch.

Ongoing ownership

After launch, teams maintain workflows by reviewing permissions, adjusting logic as systems evolve, and periodically validating assumptions.

Change management

As upstream tools change, workflows may require updates to maintain accuracy.

Periodic reviews

Regular check-ins help ensure workflows remain aligned with business needs.
