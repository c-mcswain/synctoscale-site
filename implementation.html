Discovery & scoping

Understand the systems and events that matter most. Implementation begins with identifying systems, sources of truth, and the outcomes workflows should produce.

Identify sources and events

Determine which systems emit events (e.g., CRM status changes, employee records, ticket escalations) that will trigger workflows.

Clarify ownership

Define which teams and roles are accountable for each system and workflow outcome.

Integration setup

Attach systems using scoped credentials or OAuth flows. Validate connections early to ensure workflows operate reliably.

Connect with least privilege

Use service accounts or scoped tokens. Avoid full-admin credentials where possible.

Verify connection health

Confirm successful authorization and health status before building workflows.

Workflow validation

Prior to rollout, confirm mapping rules, triggers, and edge conditions behave as expected using staging or test data.

Mapping & guardrails

Map fields explicitly, validate conditions, and place guardrails where needed.

Test with real scenarios

Use representative datasets from staging environments for early validation.

Controlled rollout

Once validated, workflows are enabled gradually with monitoring and logging so teams can observe outcomes and adjust logic if needed.

Stage-based rollout

Enable workflows for a subset of users or accounts, then expand coverage.

Monitor & adjust

Use execution logs and notifications to verify behavior and catch anomalies.
