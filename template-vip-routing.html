VIP Customer Routing

Template

Automatically route tickets from your highest-value accounts to the right team, channel, and owner — before they wait in the general queue.

Category: Support & Success Difficulty: Beginner Trigger: New support ticket Systems: Helpdesk · CRM · Slack

Overview

The VIP Customer Routing template watches for new tickets in your helpdesk (Zendesk, Intercom, Help Scout, etc.), looks up account context in your CRM, and routes VIP traffic to a dedicated queue and Slack channel. Nova adds extra context like ARR, plan type, and recent NPS so your team has full situational awareness from the first reply.

When to use this template

  • You have an internal or contracted SLA for strategic / VIP accounts.
  • VIP contacts are sometimes missed or picked up too slowly.
  • Your CSMs want to be looped in when their accounts file tickets.
  • You want a simple, explainable routing rule set with Nova catching edge cases.

What this template automates

  • Detects if the requester belongs to a VIP account in your CRM.
  • Applies high-priority and “VIP” tags to the ticket.
  • Routes the ticket to a specific group / inbox (e.g., Tier 2 – VIP).
  • Posts a rich summary to a dedicated Slack channel.
  • Notifies the owning CSM or AE with direct mentions.

How it works

  1. A ticket is created in the helpdesk.
  2. SyncToScale matches the requester email to a contact in the CRM.
  3. Nova classifies the account as VIP, Strategic, Standard, or Low-touch.
  4. VIP tickets are re-routed to the VIP queue and tagged accordingly.
  5. A Slack notification goes to #vip-support with account and ticket details.
  6. The owning CSM is @-mentioned if one exists in the CRM.

Workflow steps

  1. Step 1 — Trigger: New ticket in helpdesk

    Triggered from your helpdesk connector on ticket.created. The raw payload includes requester email, subject, body, and base metadata.

  2. Step 2 — Contact & account lookup

    SyncToScale queries the CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.) using the requester’s email and fetches account-level fields like ARR, plan, segment, and lifecycle stage.

    Behind the scenes — Nova enrichment

    • Resolves email aliases (e.g., support@vip.com) to the correct account.
    • Highlights if the account has multiple records with the same domain.
    • Suggests which account to treat as canonical when duplicates exist.

  3. Step 3 — VIP classification

    A simple rule-based classifier labels the ticket as VIP when any of the following are true:

    • arr >= 50000 (or your configured threshold)
    • segment IN ["VIP", "Strategic", "Enterprise"]
    • planTier = "Enterprise"

  4. Step 4 — Priority & queue update

    VIP tickets are set to High or Urgent priority, tagged as vip, and routed to your chosen group (e.g., group_id = 123456 in Zendesk).

  5. Step 5 — Slack & CSM notification

    SyncToScale posts to a central #vip-support Slack channel with a contextual summary. If an owning CSM is present in the CRM and mapped to Slack, they’re @-mentioned directly.

Field mappings

Helpdesk → CRM lookup

Source (Helpdesk) Transform Target (CRM)
ticket.requester.email Normalize to lowercase Contact.Email
ticket.requester.domain Strip subdomains (e.g., eu.vip.com → vip.com) Account.Website / Account.Domain__c

CRM → Helpdesk updates

Source (CRM) Transform Target (Helpdesk)
Account.AnnualRecurringRevenue__c Format as currency for Nova summary ticket.custom_fields.arr
Account.Segment__c Uppercase ticket.tags += ["segment-{{Segment__c}}"]
Account.Owner__r.Email Map to Slack user (via SyncToScale directory) Used in Slack @ mention
Account.Plan_Tier__c None ticket.custom_fields.plan_tier

Error handling

Common error scenarios for this template include CRM lookup failures, ambiguous domain matches, and Slack user mapping issues.

  • CRM lookup failures downgrade to standard routing and log a Nova warning.
  • Ambiguous matches (multiple accounts for one domain) go to a safe fallback queue.
  • Slack user mapping failures fall back to posting without direct @ mention.

Nova insights for this template

  • Shows distribution of VIP vs. non-VIP tickets over time.
  • Highlights VIP tickets that breached SLA even with routing in place.
  • Surfaces accounts filing unusually high ticket volumes for their ARR.
  • Recommends segment / SLA tweaks based on real-world patterns.

Set up this template in SyncToScale

  1. Go to Templates > Library and select VIP Customer Routing.
  2. Connect your helpdesk (Zendesk, Intercom, etc.) and CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, etc.).
  3. Configure your VIP rules (ARR threshold, segments, plan tiers).
  4. Map helpdesk groups and queues to the “VIP queue” slot in the template.
  5. Connect Slack and configure the destination channel and CSM mapping.
  6. Test with a non-production account, then enable the template in Active mode.

Template summary

  • Primary trigger: New ticket created
  • Runtime: Real-time (seconds)
  • Best for: B2B SaaS, support & CS teams
  • Owner: Support Ops · RevOps

Best practice defaults

  • ARR threshold: $50k+ per year.
  • Segments: “VIP”, “Strategic”, “Enterprise”.
  • Single VIP Slack channel per region or product line.
  • Regular Nova reviews of mis-routed tickets.

Nova will help you…

  • Spot accounts that should be VIP but aren’t configured as such.
  • Understand which VIPs generate the most urgent tickets.
  • Summarize VIP ticket patterns before renewals or QBRs.
